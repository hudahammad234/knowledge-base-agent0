HR POLICY DOCUMENT

Section 1: Recruitment and Onboarding
All new employees undergo a structured 5-day onboarding program covering company
culture, tools, security training, and role-specific shadowing. A 90-day probation
period applies to all new hires.

Section 2: Compensation and Benefits
Salaries are reviewed annually in Q1. Employees are eligible for a performance-based
bonus of up to 15% of annual base salary. The company provides private health
insurance covering the employee and up to 3 dependents.

Section 3: Employee Benefits Summary
Benefits include: private health insurance, annual leave (14 days), sick leave
(10 days), maternity leave (70 days), paternity leave (3 days), a yearly training
budget of 500 JOD per employee, and a transportation allowance of 50 JOD/month.

Section 4: Termination and Resignation
Employees must provide 30 days written notice to resign. The company may terminate
employment with cause immediately or without cause with 30 days notice or payment
in lieu of notice.

Section 5: Code of Conduct
Employees are expected to act with integrity, avoid conflicts of interest, and
report any violations of the code of conduct to HR or via the anonymous ethics
hotline.
